## The Problem

The recommendation workflow needed a better understanding of user intent, but raw behavioral signals were messy and spread across multiple sources. The challenge was to turn those signals into stable classification outputs without creating an offline-only solution that never survived integration.

That pushed the work toward practical NLP: careful feature shaping, operational validation, and service boundaries that product teams could actually consume.

## Approach

The most important part of the work was reducing integration friction. A model can look promising in isolation and still fail if its interface, error behavior, or data assumptions are too fragile. I treated validation as part of the product surface, not a separate research exercise.

That led to building reusable database clients and service interfaces alongside the classification work. The outcome was a cleaner path from experimentation to deployment.

## Key Learnings

- Production NLP quality depends as much on interfaces and validation as on model architecture.
- Multi-source data pipelines need normalization discipline before modeling pays off.
- Reusable integration clients create leverage far beyond a single model.
